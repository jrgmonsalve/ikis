# UF-010: Create a Recurring Payment

## Related User Story

US-010: Create a Recurring Payment

## Goal

Allow a family member to create a recurring payment to track future financial obligations.

## Main Flow

1. The family member opens the recurring payments section.
2. The system shows the list of recurring payments.
3. The family member selects "Create recurring payment".
4. The system shows the recurring payment form.
5. The family member enters the recurring payment name.
6. The family member enters the expected amount.
7. The family member selects the frequency.
8. The family member selects the next due date.
9. The family member optionally selects a suggested account.
10. The family member optionally selects a suggested category.
11. The family member submits the form.
12. The system validates the recurring payment information.
13. The system creates the recurring payment as active.
14. The system shows the recurring payment as an upcoming obligation.

## Alternative Flows

### Alternative Flow 1: Missing name

1. The family member submits the form without a name.
2. The system does not create the recurring payment.
3. The system shows a validation message.
4. The family member enters a valid name.

### Alternative Flow 2: Missing expected amount

1. The family member submits the form without an expected amount.
2. The system does not create the recurring payment.
3. The system shows a validation message.
4. The family member enters a valid amount.

### Alternative Flow 3: Missing frequency

1. The family member submits the form without selecting a frequency.
2. The system does not create the recurring payment.
3. The system shows a validation message.
4. The family member selects a frequency.

### Alternative Flow 4: Missing next due date

1. The family member submits the form without a next due date.
2. The system does not create the recurring payment.
3. The system shows a validation message.
4. The family member selects a due date.

## Postconditions

* A recurring payment is created.
* The recurring payment appears as an upcoming obligation.
* No expense transaction is created until the user marks the recurring payment as paid.
