# Personal and Family Finance App - Business Rules

## BR-001: Authenticated Access Required

### Title

Authenticated access required

### Business Rule

A user must be authenticated before accessing any family financial information.

### Details

* Unauthenticated users cannot access families.
* Unauthenticated users cannot access accounts.
* Unauthenticated users cannot access categories.
* Unauthenticated users cannot access transactions.
* Unauthenticated users cannot access budgets.
* Unauthenticated users cannot access recurring payments.
* Unauthenticated users cannot access reports or dashboards.

### Reason

Financial information is private and must only be visible to authenticated users.

---

## BR-002: Family Data Isolation

### Title

Family data isolation

### Business Rule

A user can only access financial data that belongs to a family where the user is an active member.

### Details

* A user cannot access a family unless they are a member of that family.
* A user cannot access accounts from another family.
* A user cannot access categories from another family.
* A user cannot access transactions from another family.
* A user cannot access budgets from another family.
* A user cannot access recurring payments from another family.
* Reports and dashboards must only use data from the selected family.

### Reason

Each family represents an isolated financial workspace.

---

## BR-003: Family Owner Permissions

### Title

Family owner permissions

### Business Rule

The family owner has full control over the family workspace.

### Details

* The family owner can invite new members.
* The family owner can remove members from the family.
* The family owner can manage family settings.
* The family owner can create, update, and deactivate accounts.
* The family owner can create, update, and deactivate categories.
* The family owner can create, update, and deactivate budgets.
* The family owner can create, update, and deactivate recurring payments.
* The family owner can view all family transactions and reports.
* The family owner cannot be removed by another member.

### Reason

Each family must have one user responsible for administration and ownership.

---

## BR-004: Account Balance Calculation

### Title

Account balance calculation

### Business Rule

An account balance must be calculated from the initial balance and the transactions associated with that account.

### Details

* The initial balance is used as the starting point.
* Income transactions increase the account balance.
* Expense transactions decrease the account balance.
* Transfers decrease the source account balance.
* Transfers increase the destination account balance.
* The current balance must always reflect the latest valid transactions.
* Reports and dashboards must use the calculated balance.

### Reason

The system must keep balances consistent and auditable.

---

## BR-005: Credit Card Balance Handling

### Title

Credit card balance handling

### Business Rule

Credit card accounts must be handled as owed money, not as available money.

### Details

* A credit card account can be used to register expenses.
* Expenses made with a credit card increase the owed balance.
* Payments made to the credit card reduce the owed balance.
* Credit card owed balance must not be included in total available money.
* Credit card activity can be shown separately in reports and dashboards.

### Reason

A credit card does not represent available money. It represents debt generated by purchases.

---

## BR-006: Category Uniqueness per Family

### Title

Category uniqueness per family

### Business Rule

A family cannot have two active categories with the same name.

### Details

* Category names must be unique inside the same family.
* Two different families can have categories with the same name.
* Category comparison should ignore uppercase and lowercase differences.
* Inactive categories should not block the creation of a new category with the same name, unless the system supports reactivation.

### Reason

Duplicate categories create confusion in reports, budgets, and transaction classification.

---

## BR-007: Transactions Must Belong to a Family

### Title

Transactions must belong to a family

### Business Rule

Every transaction must belong to one family and must only use accounts and categories from that same family.

### Details

* A transaction must be created inside a specific family.
* The selected account must belong to the same family.
* The selected category must belong to the same family.
* For transfers, both source and destination accounts must belong to the same family.
* A transaction cannot mix accounts or categories from different families.

### Reason

Transactions must remain consistent with family data isolation. Mixing financial data between families would create incorrect balances and reports.

---

## BR-008: Transfer Is Not Income or Expense

### Title

Transfer is not income or expense

### Business Rule

A transfer between accounts must not be treated as income or expense.

### Details

* A transfer reduces the source account balance.
* A transfer increases the destination account balance.
* A transfer must not increase total family income.
* A transfer must not increase total family expenses.
* Transfers must be excluded from expense reports.
* Transfers must be excluded from income reports.
* Transfers must not affect budget usage.

### Reason

A transfer only moves money between accounts. It does not represent new money received or money spent.

---

## BR-009: Budget Usage Calculation

### Title

Budget usage calculation

### Business Rule

A budget usage must be calculated only from expense transactions that match the budget category and budget period.

### Details

* Only expense transactions affect budget usage.
* Income transactions must not affect budget usage.
* Transfer transactions must not affect budget usage.
* The expense category must match the budget category.
* The expense transaction date must be inside the budget period.
* The spent amount is the sum of all matching expenses.
* The remaining amount is calculated as planned amount minus spent amount.
* If spent amount is greater than planned amount, the budget is exceeded.

### Reason

Budgets are intended to control spending, not income or money transfers.

---

## BR-010: Budget Period Validation

### Title

Budget period validation

### Business Rule

Every budget must have a valid period.

### Details

* A budget can be monthly.
* A budget can be yearly.
* A budget can use a custom date range.
* A monthly budget must have a month and year.
* A yearly budget must have a year.
* A custom budget must have a start date and an end date.
* For custom budgets, the start date must be before the end date.

### Reason

The system needs a clear period to calculate budget usage correctly.

---

## BR-011: Budget Uniqueness per Family

### Title

Budget uniqueness per family

### Business Rule

A family should not have duplicate budgets for the same category and period.

### Details

* A family cannot have two active budgets for the same category and same monthly period.
* A family cannot have two active budgets for the same category and same yearly period.
* A family cannot have two active budgets for the same category and exact same custom date range.
* Different families can have similar budgets.
* Different categories can have budgets for the same period.
* A completed budget can be copied to create a new budget for a later period.
* Copying preserves the name, category, planned amount, and currency.
* Monthly copies default to the following month, including the year rollover from December to January.
* Yearly copies default to the following year.
* Custom-period copies require the user to select a new start date and end date.
* A copied budget is a new budget and does not inherit spent amount, remaining amount, percentage used, or matched transactions.
* The copied budget remains subject to the duplicate category-and-period validation.

### Reason

Duplicate budgets for the same category and period would create confusion in calculations and reports.

---

## BR-012: Recurring Payment Does Not Automatically Create an Expense

### Title

Recurring payment does not automatically create an expense

### Business Rule

A recurring payment must not create an expense automatically unless the user confirms or registers the payment.

### Details

* A recurring payment represents an expected obligation.
* The system may show it as pending.
* The system may remind the user about it.
* The system must not reduce the account balance automatically before payment confirmation.
* The system must not affect reports or budgets until an expense is created.

### Reason

A pending payment is not the same as a real paid expense.

---

## BR-013: Marking a Recurring Payment as Paid Creates an Expense

### Title

Marking a recurring payment as paid creates an expense

### Business Rule

When a recurring payment is marked as paid, the system must create an expense transaction.

### Details

* The user must confirm the payment amount.
* The user must confirm or select the account used to pay.
* The user must confirm or select the category.
* The system creates an expense transaction.
* The selected account balance is reduced.
* The expense is included in reports.
* The expense is included in budget calculations.
* The recurring payment is marked as paid for the current period.
* The system updates the next due date according to the recurring payment frequency.

### Reason

Once paid, the recurring payment becomes a real financial transaction.

---

## BR-014: Reports Must Use Only Valid Transactions

### Title

Reports must use only valid transactions

### Business Rule

Reports and dashboards must be calculated using valid transactions from the selected family.

### Details

* Expense reports must include only expense transactions.
* Income reports must include only income transactions.
* Transfer transactions must not be counted as income or expenses.
* Budget reports must use only expense transactions that match the budget category and period.
* Account balance reports must use valid transactions associated with each account.
* Reports must only use data from the selected family.

### Reason

Reports must reflect accurate financial information.

---

## BR-015: Total Available Balance Excludes Credit Card Debt

### Title

Total available balance excludes credit card debt

### Business Rule

The total available balance must include only accounts that represent available money.

### Details

* Savings accounts are included in total available balance.
* Cash accounts are included in total available balance.
* Digital wallet accounts are included in total available balance.
* Credit card accounts are not included as available money.
* Credit card owed balances may be shown separately.
* Total available balance must be calculated from the current balance of eligible accounts.

### Reason

Credit cards represent owed money, not available money.

---

## BR-016: Financial Data Must Be Scoped by Selected Family

### Title

Financial data must be scoped by selected family

### Business Rule

When a user belongs to more than one family, all financial actions must be performed inside the currently selected family.

### Details

* Account creation must be associated with the selected family.
* Category creation must be associated with the selected family.
* Transactions must be associated with the selected family.
* Budgets must be associated with the selected family.
* Recurring payments must be associated with the selected family.
* Reports and dashboard data must be calculated from the selected family only.

### Reason

A user may participate in different financial groups, and their data must not be mixed.

---

## BR-017: Transactions Should Preserve Historical Accuracy

### Title

Transactions should preserve historical accuracy

### Business Rule

Transactions should keep the financial data that was valid at the time they were created.

### Details

* A transaction should keep its original amount.
* A transaction should keep its transaction date.
* A transaction should keep its selected account.
* A transaction should keep its selected category.
* If a category is later renamed, historical reports should remain understandable.
* If an account is later deactivated, historical transactions should remain available.

### Reason

Financial history must remain reliable even if accounts or categories change later.

---

## BR-018: Deactivation Preferred Over Deletion

### Title

Deactivation preferred over deletion

### Business Rule

Important financial entities should be deactivated instead of permanently deleted when they already have historical usage.

### Details

* Accounts with transactions should not be permanently deleted.
* Categories with transactions should not be permanently deleted.
* Budgets with historical usage should not be permanently deleted.
* Recurring payments with payment history should not be permanently deleted.
* Deactivated entities should not appear as default options for new records.
* Historical reports should still be able to reference deactivated entities.

### Reason

Permanent deletion can break historical records, balances, and reports.

---

## BR-019: Transaction Amount Must Be Positive

### Title

Transaction amount must be positive

### Business Rule

Every transaction amount must be greater than zero.

### Details

* Income amount must be greater than zero.
* Expense amount must be greater than zero.
* Transfer amount must be greater than zero.
* Negative amounts should not be allowed.
* The transaction type determines whether the balance increases or decreases.

### Reason

Using positive amounts with explicit transaction types keeps calculations simpler and less error-prone.

---

## BR-020: Transaction Date Required

### Title

Transaction date required

### Business Rule

Every transaction must have a transaction date.

### Details

* If the user provides a transaction date, the system uses that date.
* If the user does not provide a transaction date, the system uses the current date.
* Reports, budgets, and dashboards must use the transaction date, not the creation date.

### Reason

A transaction may be registered today but belong to a previous financial period.

---

# Business Rules Summary

The MVP business rules cover:

* Authentication.
* Family data isolation.
* Family owner permissions.
* Account balance calculations.
* Credit card handling.
* Category uniqueness.
* Transaction consistency.
* Transfer behavior.
* Budget calculations.
* Recurring payment behavior.
* Reporting calculations.
* Historical financial accuracy.
* Deactivation instead of deletion.
* Transaction amount and date validation.
